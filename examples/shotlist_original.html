Characters:
MARK — lean runner, mid 30s, short dark hair, salt-and-pepper stubble, skin sheened and dripping with sweat, cheeks flushed deep red, orange singlet darkened at the chest and back, race bib number 2117 crumpled at one corner. A matte-white smart water bottle with a thin LED ring at its base sits in a branded cooler stand just past the finish line.

Scene:
A city marathon finish straight, late morning. The finish arch stands behind him; a crowd leans over the barriers on both sides. The cooler stand with the white bottle is three steps past the line, right side. Mark crosses the line at a broken, exhausted stride.

CUT 1 — Wide tracking from ahead of him, 35mm, dolly back matching his stride:
Mark staggers across the finish line, arms dropping, stride breaking from run to walk. His chest heaves; he squints against the light, haze behind him. The crowd along the barriers claps and leans in. His eyes find the cooler stand — a beat of pure want.

CUT 2 — Medium on the stand, 50mm, static:
His hand enters frame and closes around the white bottle — fingers pressing into the matte surface, condensation smearing. As his palm seals the grip, the LED ring at the base wakes: a soft pulse of aqua light. A micro-pause — his thumb strokes the ring once, disbelief flickering to a tired half-smile.

CUT 3 — Close-up profile, 85mm, slow push-in:
He tips the bottle back and drinks — throat working in long swallows, a bead of water escaping down his chin and cutting a clean line through the sweat. Eyes close. Shoulders finally drop. He lowers the bottle and exhales through rounded lips, the LED ring still pulsing gently at the bottom of frame.

Characters:
MARK — the same man, mid 30s, dry and rested, the same orange singlet, clean and bright, bib not yet pinned. The matte-white smart bottle with the LED ring stands open under the club water cooler.
LENA — his older sister and coach, early 40s, weathered navy windbreaker with the club crest, stopwatch on a lanyard, tablet in one hand, reading glasses pushed up into her short grey-streaked hair.

Scene:
A running-club office before dawn, race day. A corkboard of route maps behind a scarred desk; one window going pale blue. Lena stands at the desk over her tablet, frame-left. Mark fills the white bottle at the cooler by the window, frame-right, six feet away.

CUT 1 — Medium two-shot, 35mm, static:
Water gurgles into the bottle. Lena doesn't look up from the tablet; she taps it twice with a knuckle. "Kilometer thirty is where it broke you last year." A beat. "We fixed it." Mark watches the water level rise, jaw working — the memory landing and being put away.

CUT 2 — Close on hands and bottle, 50mm, slight overhead tilt:
Mark caps the bottle and turns it once in his hand; the LED ring blinks awake — one calm aqua pulse. His thumb rests on the ring a beat longer than it needs to.

CUT 3 — Singles, 85mm, over-the-shoulder pair:
Mark looks up at Lena. A breath first — then, quiet and level: "Today I finish it." Lena finally looks up from the tablet, holds his gaze, and gives one slow, certain nod — the nod of someone who has already run the numbers. The smallest smile tugs at Mark's mouth as he slides the bottle into his belt.

Scene:
The marathon finish straight, moments after scene 1. Mark stands just past the line, crowd at the barriers on both sides. Lena pushes through the official gap in the barrier, frame-left.

Lena strides through the barrier gap, stopwatch swinging — she isn't running, she's arriving, certain. Mark sees her and his whole exhausted face opens. The crowd noise swells around them.

CUT 2 — Medium two-shot, 50mm, slow push-in:
She grips his shoulders, he grips the bottle, and they lean in — forehead to forehead, both laughing, breath ragged. She says something the crowd swallows; he nods against her forehead. The bottle rises between them, aqua ring pulsing.

CUT 3 — Reverse wide from behind them, 24mm, static:
Their two silhouettes against the haze and the arch — coach and runner, foreheads together, the bottle held between them, crowd leaning in from both edges of frame. A held, triumphant beat.
